Preparation Guide

Hiking and adventuring through parks should be fun, however there are a few things that you should be aware of. In this guide you will be taken through some of the basic requirements needed for camping and hiking.

Hiking in State Parks

Hiking in state parks is fairly easy, you really don't need to buy expensive equipment. The only real requirement is to bring money for the admission fee which ranges from $1 to $6. Other recommended items can be seen in the list below.

  1. Water Bottle
  2. Hiking Boots
  3. Sandals
  4. Swimwear
  5. Sun Screen

Finally, one basic rule that every hiker should be aware of is: "Leave nothing but footprints, take nothing but pictures".

Camping in State Parks

Camping in State Parks is slightly more complicated. Some parks will have premade camp grounds for you to use, others such as the Yellowbarn State Forest will not. I would recommend brining the following items:

  1. Tent, poles, and stakes
  2. Tarp
  3. Sleeping bags, sleeping pads
  4. Pillows
  5. Flashlights
  6. Firewood
  7. Lighter
  8. Cooking supplies
  9. Insect repellant
  10. Rainwear

Please keep in mind that this is not an exhaustive list. Before camping I would highly recommend researching both the area and supplies you need.

While camping within a state forest, please follow all New York State Laws regarding camping as seen here.
