Watkins Glen

Watkins Glen is one of the most well-known and fantastic parks in Central New York being renowned for its gorges and waterfalls. There are two primary trails that take hikers above and through the nearly 600-foot-deep gorge. Along these trails you can view over twenty waterfalls and many crystal clear pools of water. Along this trail, you can pass next to two waterfalls within arms-length of the trail.

The two trails of the park are known as the Gorge Trail and the Indian Trail. The Indian Trail is less scenic but far easier of a hike. It also gives hikers a chance to view the gorge from above. While hiking this trail, you pass next to a cemetery and some small abandoned building. In addition, there are several small overlooks.

The Gorge Trail is incredibly impressive. While following the stream, you have opportunities to pass through two small tunnels that pass through rock faces. There are also opportunities to wade through the steam bed. One the most impressive views are atop a stone bridge that is situated directly above a sizeable waterfall.

While hiking the gorge, I would recommend brining sandals along with hiking shoes for if you decided to enter the stream. I would also advise caution for elderly or low mobility people as there are 800 step stair cases.
