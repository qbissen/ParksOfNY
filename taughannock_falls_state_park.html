Taughannock Falls State Park

Taughannock Falls is the largest single drop waterfall east of the Rocky Mountains. The area surrounding the falls in an incredibly scenic and impressive gorge that is nearly 215 feet in height. While approaching the falls, one can wade through a slow moving river or choose to hike through a shaded trail. While directly approaching the waterfalls in prohibited due to falling rock danger, there is a sizable viewing area about 50feet from the falls that still gets misted from the falling water.

Taughannock got its namesake from a Delaware chief who was killed in battle near the falls and was subsequently thrown from atop the falls. It is also theorized that the falls were simply named after the natural beauty of the region.

The history of the parks begins with the acquisition of the territory following the American Revolution in which the Cayuga Tribe as forced to abandon the territory due to their allegiance with the defeated British. Throughout the early 19th century the creek was used for the creation of gunpowder and other munitions. Later, the land was purchased by New York State and in 1925 was converted into a state park.

While hiking Taughannock, hikers should bring sandals. Furthermore, hikers are cautioned to avoid approaching the falls or any area marked with "falling rock zone."
